    Why newsletter growth is a power-law game

    The average newsletter barely earns. The top decile owns the revenue. Here's the gap.

    0.62% vs 62%

    The median newsletter converts 0.62% of readers to paid. The top 10% capture 62% of all subscription revenue. Readiness is the divide.

    Subject lines decide opens

    64% of recipients decide whether to open based on the subject line alone; 69% mark spam from it. A weak line costs you the send.

    Cadence builds the habit

    Inconsistent sending kills momentum. Creators who commit to a rhythm (weekly wins for most) compound an audience that expects the next issue.

    Niche sets the ceiling

    Finance, AI/tech, and B2B newsletters command $50–150+ CPMs. A small, well-fit list in a high-CPM niche often out-earns a huge generic one.

    FAQ

    Is the audit really free?

    Yes. The on-page audit runs entirely in your browser and costs nothing. No account needed.

    Do I need to connect my newsletter?

    No. You describe your setup with a few clicks and paste recent subject lines. Newsletterly scores the strategy, not your private subscriber data — so it works before you've optimized anything.

    How is the score calculated?

    We weight subject-line strength, preview-text use, send cadence & consistency, deliverability hygiene, CTA clarity, and niche-fit / monetization potential — the signals that drive open rates and paid conversion.

    Will this guarantee I hit the top 10%?

    No tool can. But the gap between median and top-decile newsletters is mostly operational — cadence, subject lines, CTA, niche fit. Newsletterly shows you exactly where your gaps are.
