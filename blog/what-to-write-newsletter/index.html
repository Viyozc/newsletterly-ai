What to Write in a Newsletter: 12 Ideas That Keep Subscribers

Updated 2026-07-22 · Newsletterly team

The blank-page problem

Most newsletters die not from lack of subscribers but from "what do I even send?". A free newsletter growth audit scores subject, cadence, deliverability, CTA, and niche — but you still need something to say. Here are twelve proven formats.

1. One actionable tip

The simplest issue: teach one thing your reader can use today. Low effort, high value, easy to write weekly.

2. Behind-the-scenes

Show the work: what you're building, a mistake you made, a result. People subscribe to people.

3. A curated list

"3 links worth your time this week" positions you as a filter, not just a broadcaster.

4. A reader question, answered

Reply to one subscriber in the issue. It signals you read replies — and boosts reply rates.

5–12. More formats

A short case study, a before/after, a tool you use, a misconception to correct, a personal win, a resource roundup, a "what I changed my mind about", a teardown of something good, and a one-question poll. Mix them so no two issues feel the same.

Keep one clear CTA

Every issue should ask for one thing: reply, click, or buy. Burying three links dilutes all of them. A free growth audit checks whether your CTA is doing its job.
